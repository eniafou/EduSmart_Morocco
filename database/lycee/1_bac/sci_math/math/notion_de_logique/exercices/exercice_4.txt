\textbf{Exercise 4:} $x \in \mathbb{R}^+$. Montrer que:
\begin{equation*}
\frac{1}{1+\sqrt{x}} = 1-\sqrt{x} \implies x = 0
\end{equation*}

Solution:
\begin{align*}
\frac{1}{1+\sqrt{x}} = 1-\sqrt{x} &\implies (1+\sqrt{x})(1-\sqrt{x}) = 1 \\
&\implies 1 - (\sqrt{x})^2 = 1 \\
&\implies 1 + x = 1 \\
&\implies x = 0
\end{align*}
